Chicken Alfredo

Prep and Cook Time: 30 minutes

Serves 4


This version of chicken alfredo is the classic American version. Brought to the United States in the early 1900's by Italian immigrants.

Ingredients

  • 1 lbs. (450g) boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 lbs (453g) tagliatelle
  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • Cooking spray
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 c (22g) grated Parmigiano Reggiano, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 c (22g) grated Pecorino Romano
  • 1/2 cup (118ml) skim milk
  • 2 Tbls. (28g) unsalted butter
  • 1 large zucchini, diced into 1/2" pieces

Instructions

  1. Bring two large pots of water to a boil and season generously with salt.
  2. While the water comes to a boil, prep the chicken breast by cutting the in half horizontally. Pat dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper on both sides. Set aside
  3. To one pot of boiling water stir in the pasta and to the other pot add in the cauliflower florets. Cook both for 9 minutes, or untill the masta is al dente and the cauliflower is very tender. Drain both separately in a strainer and put pasta back into the pot it was boiled in.
  4. Heat a 12" pan over high heat, and grease with cooking spray. Cook each piece of chicken for 3 minutes per side, or until golden brown on both sides and the internl temperature reaches 165 Degrees F. Place on a plate and let the checken rest at least 5 minutes before slicing.
  5. Add the cauliflower to a blender with garlic, cheeses, milk, butter, salt and pepper to taste. Blend on high until very smooth. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Pour the cauliflower sauce ino the pasta and mix together until the pasta is fully coated.
  7. Heat a large skillet to medium and grease with cooking spray. When hot, add xucchini, salt and pepper to taste. Saute for 5 minutes or until tender and browned.
  8. Add about 1 c of pasta to each plate. Top with the zucchini, sliced chicken and grated Parmigiano.
